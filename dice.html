Dice

There are many types of dice used in tabletop RPGs, each with its own purpose and significance. Understanding the different types of dice and their uses can enhance your gaming experience.

Twenty Sided Dice

The twenty-sided die, or d20, is the most iconic die in tabletop RPGs. It's used for a wide variety of checks, from attack rolls to skill checks, and its results can have a significant impact on the game.

Other Dice

In addition to the d20, there are many other types of dice used in tabletop RPGs, each serving a unique purpose. These include:

  • d4 (four-sided die): Often used for determining damage for smaller weapons.
  • d6 (six-sided die): Commonly used in various games, including classic board games.
  • d8 (eight-sided die): Frequently used for weapon damage and other checks.
  • d10 (ten-sided die): Used for percentile rolls and certain class features.
  • d12 (twelve-sided die): Less common, but used for specific weapons and abilities.

Your Dungeon Master (DM) can provide guidance on which dice to use for different situations in your game.
